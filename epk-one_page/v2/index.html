Violinist · Sedona, Arizona

Tyler Carson

Fiddler on the Rock
"He lost his voice. He found his violin. And the music that came out the other side is unlike anything you've ever heard."

After years as an internationally touring musician — performing for 60,000 people at the Commonwealth Games, sharing a stage with Sandy Kelly (Johnny Cash's collaborator), and debuting as soloist with the Victoria Symphony Orchestra at thirteen — Tyler Carson hit a wall. A long depression took his voice. What came back was something deeper: a violin sound shaped by grief, recovery, and an unshakeable love for being alive.

Now featured on CBS Mornings and based in Sedona, Tyler plays in the red rock canyons with a presence that stops people cold. Not just technically exceptional — emotionally undeniable. The energy you see in his performance is not manufactured. It's what survival looks like when it picks up a bow.
