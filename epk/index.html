The Artist

One violin.
An entire orchestra.

Tyler Carson defies every expectation. Classically trained, genre-blind, and performing solo in Sedona's most breathtaking landscapes — he fuses live looping, vocals, and storytelling to create something that sounds like an entire orchestra. Led Zeppelin. Taylor Swift. Original compositions that make the red rocks feel alive.

A former child star who performed for over 250,000 people by age 11, Tyler reemerged as a visionary artist after a profound healing journey that reshaped his music and mission. That story is captured in the award-winning documentary Living Music — and told twice on CBS Mornings, including their prestigious Best of the Year issue alongside Dolly Parton.

Now based in Sedona, he leads the acclaimed concert series Fiddler on the Rock — One Man Symphony, drawing sold-out crowds Thursday evenings in the red rocks and Saturday nights at Sedona Dances Headquarters. His private violin experiences — Sedona Serenades — bring the music directly to small groups in canyon locations that most visitors never find.

The Setting

No stage. No amplification. Just Sedona.

Tyler performs in locations most visitors never find — private red rock canyons, natural formations, and the landscape itself as his stage. The silence between notes sounds different out here. So does the music.

Available for nature-centric venues, national and state parks, wellness festivals, and destination events.
