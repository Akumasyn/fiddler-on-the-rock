Two Secret Spots

Both unforgettable

An 8-year resident of Sedona, Tyler scouted the landscape to find locations chosen not just for beauty — but for what they do to sound, to light, and to the people who stand inside them.

Location One · Posse Grounds

A Hidden Viewpoint
Sedona's Natural Stage

A natural stage subtly tucked away and held by a ring of ancient junipers. Equal to any location miles inside the forest — magically located within a public park, a brief and magical stroll of just 5 minutes from your parking spot.

The kind of place most visitors to Sedona never know exists. Ancient walls, canyon expanse, and a presence that makes the violin sound like it's coming from the earth itself.

5-minute walk from parking · All experience levels
Location Two · Cultural Park

A Sweeping
Red Rock Vista

Framed by nearly all the most famous formations of Sedona — Thunder Mountain, Boynton Canyon, Cockscomb, Chimney Rock, and more. No narrow trail. Open, vast, and lit like nowhere else at golden hour.

Fully accessible, and no less stunning for it. The scale of the landscape makes Tyler's playing feel like the canyon itself is singing.

The Artist

What is Living Music?

Born of powerfully unique musical journeys — his first solo violin concert hosted in the world-renowned Pintanga Hall in Auroville, India. Celtic legends that infused his playing while touring Ireland with Sandy Kelly (Johnny Cash collaborator), visiting heritage sites like Giant's Causeway and mounds older than the pyramids.

At age 11, Tyler played fiddle tunes alongside their elder composers for 60,000 people at the Commonwealth Games. Two years later, he reinvented those same tunes as soloist to the Victoria Symphony Orchestra.

Today Tyler calls his way of playing "Living Music" — a genre born from all of it, designed not just to be heard, but to awaken something in the listener. The canyon is not his backdrop. It's his instrument.

Before You Arrive

Good to know

What to Wear

Comfortable walking shoes and a wide-brimmed hat — the sun is serious out here. Sunsets in the canyon can be cool even when the afternoon is warm. Layers are smart in spring and fall. The walk to either location is short and gentle.

What to Expect

Many guests find themselves almost hypnotized — without words, but drawn to share in the moment. Trying to find words, or reflect on feelings drawn forth like memories from another life. An inner life that quietly awakens in moments of awe. Tyler's music is designed for this — both to initiate the spark, and to expand and integrate it. Your sharing is welcome.

Best Time to Book

Midday is wonderful in fall through spring. Summer afternoon concerts are rescheduled on days above 90°F. All events are subject to weather between 55°F – 90°F. Credit and rescheduling options are offered in lieu of refunds.

Booking Policy

Standard bookings are confirmed within 24 hours. Proposals and custom events require a $50 consultation to ensure the experience is perfectly tailored — Tyler will reach out to discuss details before confirming your date.
